# Client Type — B2B SaaS

**Examples:** Enterprise SaaS, vertical SaaS, internal tools sold externally, API-first products, developer tools, ops/HR/finance platforms.

Internal Cowork examples that fit: **Loyalty Plus** (loyalty platform), **Covalonic** (B2B SaaS), enterprise integrations.

## Pillar Structure (7)

Pillars map to **buyer journey stages + decision criteria**, not regulatory categories or user benefits.

Standard mix:
1. **Use Cases by Persona** — same product, different buyer (HR / Finance / Operations / IT)
2. **Integrations** — your product + their existing stack (Salesforce, Slack, Stripe, etc.)
3. **ROI & Metrics** — concrete numbers from real customers
4. **Security & Compliance** — SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA, ISO 27001 (whichever applies)
5. **Customer Stories** — full case studies with attributed quotes
6. **Comparison & Migration** — vs major competitors, switching guide
7. **Thought Leadership** — original POV on the industry

For Loyalty Plus:
- Personas: airline ops, marketing, customer service, finance
- Integrations: PSS systems, payment gateways, email platforms
- ROI: % redemption rate increase, member-to-paid conversion
- Compliance: airline industry data standards

## Tone of Voice

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Professional, time-respecting | Casual / breezy |
| ROI-focused — quantify everything | Hype without numbers |
| "Save your team N hours per week" | "Boost productivity" |
| Acknowledge the buyer's risk | Pretend it's a no-brainer |
| Cite specific customer outcomes | Vague "trusted by leaders" |
| Speak to a busy professional | Talk down or over-simplify |

The vibe target: a more readable Stripe blog. Smart, dense with insight, respects the reader's time, never wasteful.

## Blog Post Structure

7–8 sections, ~1,500–2,500 words. Longer than other categories because B2B buyers want depth before they ever talk to sales:

1. **Hook** — name the pain point or industry tension in one sentence
2. **The cost of doing nothing** — quantify what the buyer's losing
3. **What "good" looks like** — define the standard
4. **How [Product] delivers it** — feature breakdown tied to ROI
5. **Customer outcome** — real metrics from a real customer (with named attribution if possible)
6. **What it takes to switch** — implementation, integration, training honest cost
7. **Where to learn more** — demo / case study / docs / contact sales
8. **Sources** — research, industry reports, public benchmarks

## Hero Image Style

Three categories:

1. **Dashboard / UI mockups** — your actual product UI in browser frames or laptop frames, ideally with anonymized but realistic data
2. **Charts & abstract data viz** — stylized representations of the outcome (graph going up, before/after metric)
3. **Corporate-abstract** — gradients, geometric compositions, minimal typography (for thought leadership pieces)

Master suffix shifts to:
```
modern enterprise SaaS editorial style, clean corporate aesthetic,
soft directional light, premium B2B product feel,
deep navy / electric blue palette (or product brand color),
no people unless lifestyle is core, abstract or product focus,
ultra-sharp details, photorealistic, no logos no text overlay
```

Less Hasselblad-luxury, less Apple-consumer, more Stripe / Linear / Notion enterprise.

**Critical:** Same as consumer-app — generate the surrounding context, composite real product UI in via Figma. Imagen-generated UI mockups always look fake to a buyer who knows your product.

## Video Formats (6)

B2B videos serve different jobs than consumer-app videos:

1. **Customer testimonial** — real customer on camera (or anonymized voiceover) explaining outcome
2. **ROI reveal** — counter animation rolling up to a real metric ("R12.5M recovered in year 1")
3. **Integration demo** — screen recording showing your product + their existing tool
4. **Feature → outcome** — 5 seconds of feature, 25 seconds of the business impact
5. **Founder / leadership talk** — direct-to-camera POV (~30 seconds), works for thought leadership
6. **Security / compliance reassurance** — calm, formal, address the elephant-in-the-room concern

Music: keep restrained. The default Cmaj7 underscore is perfect — don't bump BPM. B2B buyers respond to gravitas, not energy.

## Social Post Templates

Three formats rotated weekly:

- **Stat Card** — single ROI metric ("R12.5M recovered" / "98.7% uptime" / "3-week implementation")
- **Customer Quote Card** — named attribution ("CFO at [Company]")
- **Comparison Card** — your product vs status quo, in side-by-side

Plus for B2B specifically:
- **Carousel** — 5–10 slide walkthrough of an industry insight (LinkedIn carousels work extremely well for B2B)

## Distribution Channels

Service-business plus:
- **LinkedIn** is the *primary* channel (not just one of many) — captions can run 1,500+ words and perform
- **Sales enablement** — same content gets repurposed into sales decks, one-pagers, email templates
- **Industry publications** — pitch your blog posts as guest articles in Forbes, HBR, vertical pubs
- **Webinars** — repurpose blog content into 30-minute webinars; record them as gated assets
- **Podcasts** — the founder/leadership voice videos double as podcast clips

Each piece of content has 3–4 lives:
1. Blog post (long-form anchor)
2. LinkedIn article (re-formatted, slightly shorter)
3. Email newsletter to ICP list
4. Sales deck slide / talk track for AEs

## Reference Library

Different sources than other categories:
- **G2 / Capterra reviews** — real ICP language, verbatim quotes (anonymize if needed)
- **Gartner / Forrester reports** (citing public summaries, not paywalled content)
- **Customer interviews** — your own interviews are the strongest source
- **Industry benchmarks** — annual reports from the relevant industry association
- **HBR, MIT Sloan Review, McKinsey Quarterly** — for thought leadership posts
- **Public earnings calls** of customers and competitors (specific revenue numbers, growth rates)

## What's Universal vs Specific

Universal: pipeline, scripts, video framework, preview site, deploy.

B2B-SaaS-specific changes:
- Pillar names map to buyer-journey stages, not features alone
- Tone shifts to ROI-quantified professional
- Blog posts run 1,500–2,500 words (longer than service business or consumer-app)
- Hero images shift to dashboard/chart style
- LinkedIn becomes the primary channel; the other channels supplement
- Add sales enablement as a fourth "deliverable" alongside blog/video/social
- Watermark is the product wordmark + tagline

The 8-phase pipeline runs in roughly the same time budget — slightly longer on Phase 3 because B2B blogs are longer (~25% more words on average).

## Special note for Loyalty Plus / Covalonic

For the Cowork B2B properties, two additional considerations:

1. **Industry vertical content matters more than horizontal** — for Loyalty Plus, "loyalty programs in aviation" beats "loyalty programs in general"
2. **Customer logos are a primary asset** — the preview site should add a "Customers" section showing logos. Add this to build-preview.py if needed
3. **Compliance / certifications matter more for buyers** — the master plan should include a "Trust" pillar that lists certs, security posture, and uptime stats prominently
