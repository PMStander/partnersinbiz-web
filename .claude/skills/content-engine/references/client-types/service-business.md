# Client Type — Service Business

**Examples:** Law firms, accounting practices, agencies, consultancies, financial advisers, architecture firms, medical practices, immigration consultants, conveyancers, brokers.

This is the default client type — the AHS Law run is the canonical example.

## Tone of Voice

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Empathetic — clients are stressed | Cold or lecturing |
| Plain English, Grade-8 reading level | Latin maxims unexplained |
| "You" and "your" — speak directly | Third-person legalese |
| Cite the Act / Section / Standard by name | Hide behind jargon |
| Acknowledge the misconception, then correct it | Mock the reader |
| Empower with clear next steps | Leave them hanging |

## Blog Post Structure

8 sections, ~1,500 words:

1. **Hook** — name the misconception in one sentence
2. **The myth** — what most people believe
3. **The law** — actual position, cite Act + Section
4. **What you can do** — numbered list of practical steps
5. **What the other party can't do** — protections
6. **What to do if it goes wrong** — clear next action
7. **Sources & further reading** — 3–5 authoritative links
8. **CTA** — confidential consultation link

## Hero Image Style

Photoreal editorial — objects representing the legal concept:
- Brass keys (eviction, property)
- Gold pen on legal documents (contracts, employment)
- Empty wallet (debt)
- Wedding rings (marriage / divorce)
- Locked padlock (privacy)
- Children's lunchbox (maintenance)

The aesthetic is restrained, premium, "law-firm gold-on-black" or whatever the client's palette is. Hasselblad / 50mm / single key light is the canonical recipe.

## Video Formats (6)

Mix of:
- **Myth vs Fact** (high engagement — "you cannot be arrested for debt")
- **Quote / Stat reveals** (counter animation rolling up to a number)
- **List cards** ("3 things your landlord cannot do")
- **Permission matrix** ("can your boss read this?")
- **Side-by-side comparison** (silhouettes of both parties with equal weight)

Avoid: gavels, scales of justice, suited people pointing at cameras. These are clichés that signal cheap.

## Social Post Templates

Three formats rotated weekly:
- **Quote Card** — pull-quote from a blog post
- **Stat Card** — single big number + one line of context
- **Myth vs Fact** — split panel, red MYTH / gold FACT

Captions: LinkedIn long-form professional, Instagram punchy save-worthy, Facebook conversational.

## What's Universal vs Specific

The **structure** is universal across service businesses. What changes:
- Pillar names
- The Act / regulation being cited
- The reference library
- Mild tone shift (lawyers more empathetic, accountants more practical, agencies more strategic)

Everything else from the master plan + scripts + preview site works verbatim.
